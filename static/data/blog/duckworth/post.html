THE STORY BEHIND THE "EDIT" & HOW IT CAME TO BE.

To understand why I created this video, you must understand how I came to know about Kendrick Lamar first. You see, before, I wasn't very aware of the people behind the art. I did listen to music but I didn't really know much about the artist behind it, sometimes not even their name. Same with films, series, shows and movies. I watched them, enjoyed them, but didn't know the people behind them. Not the actors, not the directors, not even the publishers.

But that changed in 2024. My friend Varrow was going crazy about some beef between two artists I didn't know nor listened to. He really wanted to get my reaction on it so we watched this one disstrack together. You might already know the name, but in case you don't, it was meet the grahams.

I was honestly not very much into rap at the time, but what did surprise me were the lengths that man would go on a disstrack. It sent chills down my spine. As my first impression of him, it wasn't a good one, in the best way possible. His writing was unbelievably good. At the time, I wasn't writing much music but I had written one or two songs (which will never see the light of day), but that's pretty much how it went at first. I listened to the song, we crack a few jokes, play a few games, and carry on as usual.

However, the very next day, Varrow was asking me to react to a song again. A full 24 hours hadn't passed, yet he was here again claiming that this 'Kendrick' dude dropped ANOTHER disstrack. I was skeptical but I started the screen-share, and then proceeded to witness Not Like Us. Totally different vibe from before. It really impressed me what a wide range of music he was capable of producing.

I haven't heard of either of the two before, but as a decently curious person, I was becoming slightly interested in this guy and his music. And since YouTube has a great sense of humour, the timing couldn't have been better to place one of his songs in my recommended feed. Which happened to be arguably one of his best and my absolute favourite to this day, which is Count Me Out.

And yes, I was recommended the song, not the music video. I saw that one later. It's one of the reasons I became interested in cinematography. The song is honestly one of the most beautiful pieces of art I have ever witnessed.

Fast forward a few months of life, a few emotional breakdowns, or lack thereof, plus a binge of his entire discography, I am now a die-hard fan of Kendrick Lamar. His music got me through thick and thin. Good and bad periods of my life. The amount of inspiration he has given me simply cannot be described through words. Which is exactly why the best way to do it is through creation. Which as you might be aware of, I am a pretty big fan of.

So what medium did I choose for this creation? Why, video editing of course. I had two rather successful projects like this before, so, I wanted to make my biggest one yet about Kendrick Lamar. And so the idea was born. An edit of Kendrick.

But where my previous videos were using a single song throughout with clips matching the rhythm, I couldn't do that here. Before I was editing a character, so it was easier to have the focus on him and have the song amplify that. But how do you do that with someone who MAKES the music? You can't pick just one, right? Right.

So I decided to experiment a bit. I decided to use as many songs as I could cram in there, with different sections of the video for each. It was originally just about Kendrick, but I decided to experiment even MORE and add some cinematography in there. A few clips at home, on some random streets and at the high-school I was going to at the time (with permission) and we have a semi-coherent little timeline of events with many hidden secrets awaiting to be discovered.

What? Did you think I was going to hand everything on a single platter? You still have to do the work to piece it together. But luckily for you, you can give it a watch just a tad above this wall of text. I highly encourage you to give it a watch, I worked a whole year on it after all.

Speaking of which. I mentioned I had two successful edit projects before this one. "So how did this one do?" you might be wondering. Well, shocker, but people who watched me for edits of an anime character aren't very interested in edits about myself and some rapper they may or may not even know.

At the time of writing, it's sitting at 200 views, 2 comments that aren't mine and 8 likes (one of which is my own). It is indeed a bit soul crushing to have something you're really passionate about and have worked on for a long time fail so spectacularly, but what matters to me more than its success is the experience I got from it. I learned a lot more about both video and audio editing, as well as gained a bit of experience in shooting scenes.

And this project was actually the one where I've put the most work into the audio part. Before it was just music with the same volume for the whole duration and my whole undivided attention went into the video part, but this time I've put just as much work into the audio as I did into the video. I've used sound effects, volume tricks, mixing and matching until I got what ended up as the final result. And as a first attempt, I must say I'm quite proud of what it turned out to be.

So why did it take so long? Well, it's a problem I have with all my projects. I'm really slow with my work. I like to plan everything ahead, and the execution has to be perfect, or as close as possible to fool me into thinking it's good enough. And when it comes to the actual editing itself, I can either work for hours or minutes, it really depends on how motivated I am.

And I can't force myself to work more than I feel like, because trying to force my brain to do something it's decided it doesn't feel like only makes it want to do it even less. But when I finally do bring a project to an end, I get really happy and excited, and want to show everyone I can, because I love sharing what I create with other people, be it friends, family, or complete strangers who may or may not like what I make.

Though if you've watched the edit, previously or while reading this post, you might've noticed, even though I've mentioned that Count Me Out is my absolute favourite song of all time and what I consider one of the most beautiful pieces of art I've ever witnessed... I haven't used it in the edit.

I only had a short clip of a live performance of it at the very end. That is because I didn't feel worthy of using it. Like I said, I have never heard anything more beautiful in my life. I didn't feel like the project or my skill were high-quality enough to include Count Me Out. That is why I settled for the intro vocal and that live performance clip at the very end which I used to thank Kendrick for just being awesome.

Will I ever use Count Me Out in another project? I don't know. I hope so, but it will probably take some time, and a LOT of work.

Until then though, I'll see you in the next one.

Thanks for reading.
